Spatial Depth

Spatial depth provides a center-outward ordering of multivariate data. Points closer to the center have higher depth, while outliers have lower depth.

\( D(x; X) = 1 - \left\| \frac{1}{n} \sum S(y - x) \right\| \)

This measure forms the basis for many depth-based outlier detection methods.

Robustness

Robust statistical methods aim to remain stable under contamination and the presence of extreme observations.

Key idea: A good estimator should not be overly sensitive to a small fraction of corrupted data.
